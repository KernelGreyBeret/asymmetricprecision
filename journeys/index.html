Journeys

Enter the system from the problem you are actually carrying.

AP is not a linear course. It is a connected body of work. Journeys provide guided routes for different responsibilities, questions, and kinds of pressure.

Principle

A journey is not a funnel.

A funnel extracts attention. A journey increases understanding. These paths are designed to help explorers choose an entry point, follow relationships, and leave with a clearer mental model of the system.

Where next?

Every path should connect to the Atlas.

As AP grows, each journey will become a living route through essays, frameworks, books, laboratories, field notes, and observations. The Atlas is where those relationships become visible.

Where this connects
