# AP Design System

**Version:** 0.1  
**Purpose:** Design philosophy for Asymmetric Precision v2.0.

---

## Prime Directive

Every design choice should answer:

> Does this improve understanding?

If the answer is no, it does not belong.

---

## Design Principles

### Reveal Relationships Before Explaining Components

People understand systems by seeing relationships, not isolated parts.

### Reduce Cognitive Load

The interface should make the idea easier to hold in the mind.

### Reward Curiosity

Exploration should reveal meaningful connections, not noise.

### Make Complexity Visible

Do not hide complexity.

Structure it.

### Favor Exploration Over Navigation

Navigation helps people move through pages.

Exploration helps people move through ideas.

### Organize by Relationships

Folders are useful.

Relationships are powerful.

### Every Page Connects to Something Larger

No page should be a dead end.

Every page should answer:

- What is this?
- Why does it matter?
- How do I apply it?
- Where do I go next?

### Never Decorate. Always Communicate.

Visual elements must carry meaning.

If a line, node, label, card, or glow does not clarify something, remove it.

---

## Hidden Systems Visual Language

AP v2.0 should use a restrained version of Hidden Systems Visual Language.

Patterns may include:

- nodes
- connectors
- telemetry labels
- status indicators
- relationship lines
- system maps
- evidence callouts
- operational annotations
- layered diagrams
- signal/noise contrast

The goal is not to look futuristic.

The goal is to help people see structure.
