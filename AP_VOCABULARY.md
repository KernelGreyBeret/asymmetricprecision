# AP Vocabulary

**Version:** 0.1  
**Purpose:** Canonical language for Asymmetric Precision v2.0.

---

## Why Vocabulary Matters

Language shapes thought.

Thought shapes action.

If we want people to understand systems differently, we must first give them better language.

This vocabulary exists to create precision.

---

## Core Terms

### Applied Systems Thinking

The practical discipline of understanding, designing, operating, and improving complex systems.

### System

A collection of interconnected elements whose relationships create behavior over time.

### Hidden System

Relationships, incentives, trust, constraints, authority, culture, and feedback that are not immediately visible but shape outcomes.

### Mental Model

An internal representation of how a system behaves.

Better mental models produce better decisions.

### Domain

A major area of inquiry within Applied Systems Thinking.

Examples include leadership, complexity, trust, organizations, architecture, decision making, incentives, constraints, learning, and operational design.

### Principle

A durable truth that applies across many systems.

### Framework

A reusable method for understanding or improving systems.

### Observation

A recorded insight about how a system behaves.

Not an opinion.

An observation.

### Laboratory

A practical implementation of AP philosophy.

Examples include software tools, games, design systems, experimental interfaces, and applied prototypes.

### Atlas

The connected map of ideas, principles, frameworks, books, observations, and relationships that make up Applied Systems Thinking.

### Reflection

A learner's personal evaluation of their own understanding.

Reflection is never scored.

Only encouraged.

### Connection

A meaningful relationship between ideas.

Connections are first-class citizens.

Ideas are valuable.

Relationships make them powerful.

### Understanding

The primary measure of success.

Everything exists to increase understanding.

Nothing exists merely to increase engagement.

## Working Term for Visitors

The old web word is **user**.

AP should eventually replace that with something more precise and more human.

Candidate terms:

- Explorer
- Practitioner
- Pathfinder
- Ideonaut
- Phrononaut
- Noonaut
- Systems Pathfinder
- Cognitive Pathfinder

Status: unresolved.

Decision rule: the final term must communicate agency, curiosity, cognition, and movement through complex ideas without making the person feel mechanical, replaceable, or passive.
