Abstract

Complex systems rarely fail all at once.

They drift.
They accumulate pressure.
They hide weaknesses behind normalcy.
They adapt to survive — sometimes in ways nobody intended.

Field Notes from a Systems Architect is a collection of operational reflections, lessons, observations, and hard-earned insights gathered from years spent navigating cybersecurity, military operations, leadership, organizational dynamics, engineering, human behavior, and large-scale systems under pressure.

Rather than presenting abstract theory alone, Tommy Burke explores the lived reality of operating inside complex environments where uncertainty, incentives, trust, authority, friction, failure, and human behavior collide continuously.

Part field guide, part philosophical reflection, and part operational memoir, this book examines what complexity feels like from inside the system itself.

These are not lessons learned from perfect conditions.
They are lessons learned from reality.
