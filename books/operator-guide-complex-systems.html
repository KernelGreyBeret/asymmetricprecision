Abstract

Modern systems fail for predictable reasons — but most people are trained to see only isolated problems instead of interconnected behaviors.

In The Operator’s Guide to Complex Systems, Tommy Burke introduces a practical framework for understanding how systems actually behave in the real world.

Drawing from military operations, cybersecurity, engineering, governance, and organizational leadership, Burke breaks down concepts like feedback loops, hidden dependencies, constraints, trust relationships, failure cascades, signal versus noise, and second-order effects into clear, usable lessons.

This is not a textbook and not a management book.

It is a field guide for operators, engineers, leaders, analysts, builders, and problem-solvers who need to make decisions inside large, imperfect, constantly changing systems.

Whether the system is a network, a team, an organization, a family, or even your own habits, the same principles apply:

Everything connects.
Everything communicates.
Everything influences everything else.

The question is whether you can see it before the failure occurs.
