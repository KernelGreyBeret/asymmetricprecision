Abstract

Every system is governed by trust, whether intentionally or accidentally.

Trust, Constraints, and Control explores how organizations, technologies, policies, and people establish authority, delegate decision-making, enforce boundaries, and maintain order at scale.

Through the lens of cybersecurity architecture and operational systems design, Tommy Burke explains why control without visibility fails, why trust without constraints becomes dangerous, and why most organizations misunderstand both.

The book introduces practical ways to think about trust relationships, enforcement mechanisms, policy design, autonomy, accountability, and systemic governance in both technical and human systems.

At its core, this book asks a simple question:

Who is allowed to decide?
