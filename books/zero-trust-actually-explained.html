Abstract

Zero Trust has become one of the most misunderstood concepts in modern cybersecurity.

Buried beneath vendor marketing, compliance checklists, and buzzword-heavy presentations is a genuinely powerful architectural philosophy.

One built around explicit trust evaluation, continuous authorization, contextual decision-making, and constrained access.

In Zero Trust Actually Explained, Tommy Burke strips away the hype and explains Zero Trust the way operators, architects, engineers, and leadership teams actually need to understand it.

Rather than focusing on products, the book focuses on systems: identities, policies, decision engines, enforcement points, attributes, obligations, trust relationships, and governance models.

This is not a certification guide.
It is a conceptual field guide to how Zero Trust actually works.
