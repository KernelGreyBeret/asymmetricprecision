Abstract

Systems thinking is often taught academically, abstractly, or in ways disconnected from reality.

Operators do not have that luxury.

Systems Thinking for Operators focuses on practical systems analysis under real-world conditions: incomplete information, competing priorities, limited resources, organizational friction, human unpredictability, and operational pressure.

Using stories, examples, and frameworks drawn from cyber operations, enterprise architecture, military planning, troubleshooting, and leadership, Tommy Burke demonstrates how experienced operators identify leverage points, predict failure modes, and simplify complexity without oversimplifying reality.

This book is about learning how to think clearly when systems become unclear.
