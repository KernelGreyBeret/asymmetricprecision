# AP System Weave

## Sprint 23 — System Weave

**Connections should appear where they create understanding.**

Sprint 23 moves AP beyond bottom-of-page related links. It introduces a read-along weave that makes the surrounding system visible while someone is reading a detail page.

## What changed

- Added `assets/system/ap-system-weave.js`
- Added `assets/system/ap-system-weave.css`
- Updated `assets/system/ap-system.js`
- Updated `assets/system/ap-system.css`

## What the weave does

The weave resolves the current page against AP's content and Atlas data, then surfaces relevant connections as:

- a compact system weave rail,
- principle chips,
- operating model signals,
- journey continuation cues,
- focused Atlas links,
- read-along contextual callouts inside long-form pages.

## Why this matters

AP should not force the learner to finish a page before discovering how the idea connects.

The page should reveal supporting relationships at the moment they clarify the idea.

## Design rule

Do not turn the weave into decoration.

Every signal must help the learner understand one of three things:

1. what principle is operating,
2. what model can be applied,
3. where the idea sits in the larger AP system.
